MONKEY:

  1. Classification

    Monkeys are primates in the order of simians. The term "monkey" is a common-language term for two groups of primates: Old World monkeys and New World monkeys.

  2. Habitat

    Many monkeys live in trees, but some, like baboons, live on the ground. Old World monkeys live in Africa and Asia, while New World monkeys live in Mexico, Central and South America.

  3. Size

    The smallest Old World monkey is the talapoin, which is 13–15 in long and weighs 1.5–2.9 lb. The largest Old World monkey is the male mandrill, which is 28 in long and weighs up to 110 lb.

  4. Diet

    Most monkeys are omnivorous, but they prefer plant matter. They eat fruit, flowers, leaves, bulbs, rhizomes, insects, snails, small mammals, and garbage.

  5. Features

    New World monkeys have flatter noses with nostrils facing to the side. Most do not have full color vision, and their thumbs are not opposable.
