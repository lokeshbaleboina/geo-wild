DOG:

  1. Domestication

    Dogs were the first animals to be domesticated by humans, over 14,000 years ago.

  2. Appearance

    Dogs come in many shapes, sizes, and colors. They have powerful jaws with around 42 teeth and well-developed senses of smell, hearing, and sight.

  3. Communication

    Dogs communicate through eye gaze, facial expressions, vocalizations, body posture, and scents.

  4. Behavior

    Dogs are known for being loyal, friendly, and playful. They are also known for being bold, aggressive, and hypersocial.

  5. Uses

    Dogs are used for many purposes, including as pets, police dogs, army dogs, assistance dogs, and more.

  6. Population

    The global dog population is estimated to be 700 million to 1 billion.

  7. Breed categories

    Dog breeds are sometimes grouped into similar types, such as Mastiff types, spitz types, pit bulls, or Lurchers.

  8. Docking and declawing

    Docking is a procedure that involves cropping a dog's tail, and it is often performed for cosmetic reasons. Declawing is a procedure that involves removing a dog's claws to prevent injuries.

  9. Perception

    Dogs have a particularly strong sense of smell compared to humans. They also have the ability to sense the Earth's magnetic field.
