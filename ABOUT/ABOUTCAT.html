CAT:

  1. Classification

    Cats are small, carnivorous mammals in the Felidae family. They are the only domesticated species in this family.

  2. Appearance

    Cats have slender, muscular bodies, retractile claws, and strong forelimbs. Their fur can be short or long, and some species have all-black fur.

  3. Behavior

    Cats are social but hunt alone, and are most active during twilight. They communicate with vocalizations like meowing, purring, hissing, and growling, as well as body language.

  4. Domestication

    Cats have been domesticated for almost 10,000 years. They are popular pets, kept for hunting mice and rats, and as companions.

  5. Breeding

    Female domestic cats can have kittens from spring to late autumn in temperate zones, and throughout the year in equatorial regions. Litter sizes are usually two to five kittens.
