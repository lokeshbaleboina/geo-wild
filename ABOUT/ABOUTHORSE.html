  2. Domestication

    Humans began domesticating horses around 4000 BCE, and by 3000 BCE domestication was widespread.

  3. Uses

    Horses are used for many purposes, including riding, transportation, carrying things, pulling carts, and helping in agriculture. They are also used in equestrianism and equine sports such as cross-country, show jumping, and dressage.

  4. Breeds

    There are more than 300 breeds of horse in the world today, which are loosely divided into three categories based on temperament: hot bloods, cold bloods, and warmbloods.

  5. Lifespan

    Horses have an average lifespan of between 25 and 30 years.

  6. Anatomy

    Horses have a small intestine that is 15 to 21 meters long and holds 38 to 45 liters.

  7. Limbs

    The range of motion and propulsion power of a horse varies significantly based on the placement of muscle attachment to bone.

  8. Behavior

    Horses live in bands, which are led by a dominant mare. The composition of bands changes as young animals are driven out and join other bands, or as stallions challenge each other for dominance.
